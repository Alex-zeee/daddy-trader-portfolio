08 — Blog

Trading Insights

Smart Money Concepts: How Institutions Really Move Gold

SMC · 6 min read

Retail traders lose because they trade where liquidity is engineered, not where it's consumed. Smart Money Concepts (SMC) flips the map: instead of chasing breakouts, you learn to identify order blocks — the footprints institutions leave when they accumulate positions.

On Gold, the pattern repeats daily: price sweeps a session high or low (a liquidity grab), then reverses sharply from an order block. If you mark the previous day's high/low and the Asian session range, you'll see how often London and New York hunt those levels before the real move begins.

The three pillars I teach at Daddy Trader Academy: market structure (higher highs/lows and when they shift), liquidity (where stop losses cluster), and order blocks (the last opposing candle before an impulsive move). Master these three and the chart stops looking random.

The Engulfing Setup: My Highest-Probability Gold Entry

Price Action · 5 min read

An Engulfing candle alone means nothing. An Engulfing candle at the right location is one of the most reliable entries in Gold trading. The difference is context.

My checklist: (1) price is at a key level — an order block, previous day high/low, or a fresh supply/demand zone; (2) a liquidity sweep just happened — a wick beyond the level that trapped traders; (3) the Engulfing candle closes with full-body conviction, engulfing the previous candle's range entirely.

Entry on the close or the 50% retrace of the Engulfing candle, stop loss beyond the sweep wick, target minimum 1:2 risk-to-reward. No level, no sweep, no trade — discipline is the strategy.

MQL5 Basics: Turning Your Strategy Into an Expert Advisor

Development · 7 min read

If your strategy has fixed rules, it can be automated. MQL5 is the language of MetaTrader 5, and an Expert Advisor (EA) is simply your rules written as code that runs on every tick.

Every EA has three core events: OnInit() runs once when the EA loads, OnTick() runs on every price update — this is where your entry logic lives — and OnDeinit() cleans up. Detecting an Engulfing candle is a few lines: compare the current closed candle's body against the previous candle's range using iOpen/iClose functions.

The part most people skip — and the reason most EAs fail — is risk management code: calculating lot size from account balance and stop distance before every order. Automation without risk control is just a faster way to lose. Backtest on quality tick data, forward-test on demo, and only then go live.
